Effects & Impact

Victims of flaming often do not tell anyone of their situation due to embarassment or fear of retribution. Some signs of victims of flaming include:

  • Isolating from friends and family
  • Declining grades
  • A refusal to attend school
  • Signs of depression and anxiety
  • Real or imagined illnesses that keep them out of school
  • Avoidance of electronics
  • Thoughts of suicide

How to stop flaming

Victims should not reply or 'feed' into the flaming by commenting or insulting the bullies, and should avoid places where flaming is prominent.

Bullies should apologise to the victims and delete their original flame bait comment or forum, and learn to control their emotions via breathing techniques. Speak words that are in consideration of others' feelings, and if behaviour does not improve, it is wise to put away their owned devices for a period of time.

Signs of cyberbullying

For parents who are worried if their children are participating in flaming, here are some signs:

  • Avoid conversations about their social media use
  • Owning multiple accounts on the same platform
  • Suddenly changing screens when an adult is nearby
  • Become uncharacteristically angry or irritable when they can't access electronics


Who should you contact when you are experiencing flaming?

Teachers can create a safe and supportive learning environment where students feel comfortable discussing their experiences, including any online harassment. This helps build trust and encourages students to seek help.

When things get too serious, it is time to get the police involved.
Police Hotline Number: 999

Friends can provide a listening ear, empathy, and understanding.

Knowing that your friends support you can provide a sense of validation and assurance. This support can boost your confidence and help you cope with difficult situations.
